THE GUIDE TO DENMARK

ALL THE SMALL THINGS THAT MATTER

YOUR ROOM IS COMPLETELY EMPTY? FRICK!

You ask yourself: What is all this furniture on the street while my room is empty? In Denmark it is very common that the accommodation you get will be unfurnished. But fear no more, as an immediate solution to that, people tend to leave their no longer needed furniture in front of the building and you are welcome to just take it.

BUT WHERE DID THE LAMP GO?

Danes feel very personal about decorating their spaces… When getting to your room for the first time, don't expect to have furniture in there, not even the lamps. But, that always gives you an opportunity to practice your building and decorating skills! Another great way to enlighten your space are candles! Big or small, candles are a nice light and atmosphere provider!

THE BEST THING ABOUT DENMARK - HYGGE.

Hygge? You mean a hug? Wait, what? Well, it can be a hug, but hygge also comes in many different forms. It is probably the most popular Danish word that is widespread across the world. It doesn't have a specific translation, but it means coziness and having a great time.

THE WHEELS ON THE BIKE GO ROUND AND ROUND...

Wheels on the BIKE go round and round… Yes, you read it correctly. Denmark is known for having cycling as one of the main means of transport. No matter the weather or mood, bikes are always present on the streets and other people in traffic (drivers and pedestrians) are obligated to respect the special paths for bicycles. It is also a great way to stay fit and healthy!

WHY ARE DANISH FLAGS EVERYWHEREEEEEE?!

It might seem a bit weird to see Danish flags everywhere we go. To dispel any assumptions - it is no nationalistic act. Danes use their flag to celebrate so many things: birthdays, national holidays, confirmations, even just to decorate their homes for everyday coziness. I mean, who wouldn't? Such a simple and beautiful flag (not to forget - the oldest one in the world) needs to be seen everywhere!
